About the Project

Guria, a grassroots anti-trafficking NGO based in Varanasi, India, is partnering with 100s of students across the globe to create a tidal wave of awareness, empowerment, and creativity on March 18th, 2015. 100s of students around the world are acting together in solidarity: each learning about human trafficking, creating art about trafficking, exchanging their art with each other, and finally putting on student art exhibits in their respective communities that contain a mix of student art from all over the world on March 18th.

We are students, we are Guria, we are leaders, we are 100s strong, and we demand FREEDOM NOW for the victims of human trafficking.

March 18th, 2015. 100s of students Worldwide. Guria, a grassroots non-profit organization based in Varanasi, India, is partnering with 100s of students and organizations across the globe to make art, exchange art, and display art—all on the topic of human trafficking—to empower youth around the world and create an tidal wave of awareness. On March 18th, after having learned about human trafficking, created art, and participated in the global exchange, each participating group will put on an art exhibit in their respective community of local and international student art, contributing to a day-long global demonstration of creativity and solidarity.

What is Human Trafficking?
 Human trafficking: The second largest – and fastest growing – criminal industry in the world, afflicting millions of communities around the world. It is one of the most atrocious violations of human rights of our time with an estimated 1.2 million children trafficked into various forms of slavery each year. Learn more about what we are fighting against here.
What is Guria?
 “26 years. 526 victims rescued. 589 cases against 989 human traffickers. 1 child prostitution free red light area. 3 centers for children. Countless prevention workshops. A holistic and innovative approach. Varanasi, India. Learn more about Guria—the anti-trafficking grassroots organization making this event happen—here.
Why 100s?
 100 is a significant number because it refers to a report released by Guria in 2014 entitled 100 Cases of Police Misconduct in Sex Trafficking, Child Prostitution, and Rape. We hope the worldwide mobilization of 100s of students for 100 Cases will demand that the world notice the atrocities in these 100 cases and advocate for real action to be taken against the police misconduct that allows trafficking to flourish.

We are 100s of students for 100 Cases.

Get Involved!

1: educate

Inform yourself! Learn more about trafficking in our “What is trafficking section.” Then teach about trafficking in your classroom, or raise awareness amongst your friends. Need help explaining trafficking t your community or classroom? You can Download Guria’s sample curriculum!

2: use the exchange

Create art with your students, your friends, your classmates! Guria has provided further information on how to start creating art. Then use this website to join the exchange! Share that art with other participating schools by uploading it! Then globalize your own exhibit by browsing other student’s art and selecting pieces to download and print, to add to your own exhibit on March 18th.

3: put on an exhibit

On March 18th, 2015, join the worldwide movement of 100s of students and groups across the world. Put on an exhibit in your own community!
