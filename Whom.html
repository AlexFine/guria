About Guria

Based in Varanasi, India, Guria is a grassroots organization dedicated to fighting human trafficking and commercial sexual exploitation.

Vision: To build a just and humane world where all beings co-exist in harmony.

Mission: To fight against human trafficking and forced prostitution, especially among women and children.


Guria organizes rescue missions and provides comprehensive legal aid and rehabilitation services to victims. Since i’s inception more than 20 years ago, Guria has rescued 442 minor girls from brothels and sex traffickers, achieved 329 bail rejections for human traffickers, and are currently pursuing 375 criminal cases and 196 paralegal cases against 1000+ human traffickers.

Guria is involved in prevention work with two non-formal education centers in the red light areas of Varanasi and Mau and a boat school on the Ganges river that provide supportive environments for at-risk children and children of sex workers with art therapy, vocational training, and more.

Guria also runs numerous rural women and children empowerment programs and sensitization programs with police, lawyers, and other stakeholders throughout Uttar Pradesh state, and is highly involved in government advocacy work.


FREEDOM NOW Awareness Campaign:The March 18th FREEDOM NOW Global Art Exchange & Exhibit is a part of Guria’s ongoing worldwide campaign, “FREEDOM NOW,” that raises awareness about modern-day slavery through music, art, marches, and media. Past FREEDOM NOW events have included a worldwide concert series, concerts by marginalized artists, a bike race in the UK, and much more.
